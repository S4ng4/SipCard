A SipCard for Everyone

Helping Wines Speak for Themselves
Born in the dining room, designed for every table – SipCard helps staff, guests, and wine lovers understand and enjoy wine without pressure or pretense.

The Problem I Faced

Before working on SipCard, I spent years in hospitality – first as a dining consultant and then as a manager. I often saw guests hesitate when looking at the wine list. Not because they lacked interest – but because they lacked information. Even passionate staff sometimes struggled to tell the right story at the right moment.

It was never about selling more bottles. It was about helping people feel confident, curious, and understood when choosing wine.

The Birth of SipCard

SipCard started as a personal solution. I needed a way to let wine speak for itself – no pressure, no confusion, just clarity. Each card connects to a digital wine profile: grapes, producer, food pairings, tasting notes, serving suggestions – everything in one place, one tap away.

With SipCard, your team gains a new ally – and your guests feel empowered to explore. That changed everything.

Designed for Learning First

Before boosting sales, SipCard aimed to support education. For staff who want to deepen their knowledge. For customers who love to learn. For producers who want their work to be understood and valued.

Wine is culture. SipCard is a simple tool to help share it – one card at a time.

From Simplicity to Innovation

After seeing how SipCard made a difference, I realized there was more we could do. Helping guests discover wine is great – but what if we could go deeper? Into transparency, authenticity, and emotional connection?

That’s when the idea of SipTag came to life – the natural evolution of the project. Built with NFC technology, designed for smart inventory, traceability, and storytelling at scale. Imagine an elegant card next to a bottle: the guest taps their phone, and all the information appears – like magic.

Low Tech, High Impact. Tap into the future.

SipCard was born from the floor of a restaurant – and built for every table
